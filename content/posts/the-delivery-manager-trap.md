---
title: The Delivery Manager Trap
tagline: Why "unblocking the team" can become the whole job
date: 2024-11-10
---

There's a version of technical delivery management that looks productive and isn't.

It goes like this: you run the standups, you chase the dependencies, you update the JIRA board, you write the status report, you attend the steering committee. You are *busy*. The team is shipping. Everything looks fine.

And then six months later you look up and realise you have no idea what the team is actually building, whether it's the right thing, or whether any of it will matter.

### The trap

The delivery manager trap is optimising for throughput over outcomes. It feels responsible — you're keeping things moving, clearing blockers, protecting the team's time. But motion is not progress.

The hardest part of the role is resisting the pull of the tactical. Tactical work is satisfying. It has clear inputs and outputs. You can point to it. Strategic work is uncomfortable — ambiguous, slow, often invisible until it's too late.

### Getting out

The way out is to deliberately carve time for the uncomfortable questions:

- Are we building the right thing?
- Does the team understand why it matters?
- What's the one thing, if it went wrong, that would make this whole effort pointless?

These questions don't fit neatly into a standup or a sprint ceremony. They require a different kind of attention — slower, less structured, harder to schedule.

But they're the job. The tickets are just the tickets.
